About the Destination

Mexico's Diving Paradise

Cozumel is a Caribbean island off Mexico's Yucatán Peninsula, widely considered one of the top scuba diving and snorkeling destinations on Earth. Jacques Cousteau introduced the world to Cozumel's reefs in a 1961 documentary.

The island sits along the Mesoamerican Barrier Reef System, the second-largest coral reef in the world. The famous Palancar Reef offers wall dives, drift dives, and shallow snorkeling for every level.

Beyond the reef, Cozumel offers a charming downtown (San Miguel), white-sand beaches, fresh seafood, colorful street art, and warm, welcoming locals.

Insider Knowledge

Tips From Our Cozumel Guides

☀️
Book morning tours
Visibility is best before afternoon winds. Our guides always recommend early starts.
🪸
Don't touch the reef
Coral is living and fragile. Even a gentle touch can damage decades of growth.
🌊
Embrace the drift
Cozumel's drift diving uses the natural current to glide along the reef wall effortlessly.
💧
Stay hydrated
The Caribbean sun is intense. Drink water before and after diving to avoid decompression risk.
🍽️
Eat at local spots
Head to downtown San Miguel for the freshest ceviche at a fraction of resort prices.
🦟
Use reef-safe sunscreen
Regular sunscreen is banned in Cozumel — it's harmful to the coral ecosystem.
