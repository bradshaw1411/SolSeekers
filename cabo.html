About the Destination

Land's End on the Baja Peninsula

Cabo San Lucas sits at the very tip of Mexico's Baja California Peninsula, where the Pacific Ocean and the Sea of Cortez collide in dramatic fashion. The result is a destination unlike any other: rugged desert cliffs, turquoise coves, and an electrifying marine ecosystem.

The crown jewel is El Arco, a natural stone arch rising from the sea at Land's End, flanked by sea lion colonies. It's one of the most photographed landmarks in all of Mexico.

Beyond the scenery, Cabo is a playground for adventure seekers — world-class sportfishing, snorkeling, whale watching, ATV rides, and sunset catamaran cruises.

Insider Knowledge

Tips From Our Cabo San Lucas Guides

🌊
Respect Pacific waves
Pacific-facing beaches have dangerous currents. Always swim on Sea of Cortez side beaches like Médano.
🌅
Book sunset tours early
The Cabo sunset is world-famous. Catamaran spots sell out weeks in advance during peak season.
🦭
Visit sea lions by water taxi
Hire a quick water taxi from Médano Beach to El Arco and Pelican Rock for a close-up view.
🎣
Fish the corridor
The stretch between Cabo San Lucas and San José del Cabo is legendary for marlin. May–Nov is prime.
🌮
Eat like a local
Head to the Municipal Market for incredible fish tacos at a fraction of marina restaurant prices.
💳
Carry pesos
Pay in pesos at local spots — ATMs dispense pesos at fair rates.
