About the Destination

The Caribbean's Iconic Coastline

Cancún stretches along a 14-mile barrier island shaped like the number "7", with the Caribbean Sea on one side and a calm lagoon on the other — creating some of the most photogenic turquoise water anywhere on the planet.

Just offshore, the MUSA (Museo Subacuático de Arte) features over 500 submerged sculptures slowly being reclaimed by coral, creating an otherworldly underwater gallery.

Cancún is also the jump-off point for Isla Mujeres — a laid-back island just 30 minutes away with world-class snorkeling and the best ceviche in the Caribbean.

Insider Knowledge

Tips From Our Cancún Guides

🏖️
Playa Delfines is the best free beach
Public, free, and offers the most photogenic view of the Hotel Zone with brilliant turquoise water.
🚌
Use the R-1 bus
The R-1 local bus runs the entire Hotel Zone for a few pesos — safe, frequent, and far cheaper than taxis.
🎨
Book MUSA early
The underwater museum is one of Cancún's most in-demand experiences — book as soon as your trip is confirmed.
🌊
Respect red flag days
The Caribbean can turn rough quickly. Always check beach flags — red or black means stay out.
🐢
Support turtle conservation
Never use flashlights on the beach at night during nesting season — light disorients hatchlings.
🍹
Explore Downtown Cancún
El Centro is a world away from the Hotel Zone — authentic food markets and real Mexican culture at half the price.
