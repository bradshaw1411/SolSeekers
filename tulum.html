About the Destination

Where Ancient Meets Wild

Tulum is unlike any other destination in Mexico. The ancient Mayan city sits atop dramatic limestone cliffs overlooking the turquoise Caribbean Sea, its temples standing in silent testament to a civilization that flourished here over a thousand years ago.

Below the cliffs, a stretch of beach so beautiful it almost defies description. A short distance inland, the Gran Cenote and dozens of other sacred sinkholes offer some of the most magical swimming experiences on earth.

To the south, the Sian Ka'an Biosphere Reserve — a UNESCO World Heritage Site — protects 1.3 million acres of jungle, mangroves, and reef teeming with jaguars, manatees, and flamingos.

Getting There

How to Reach Tulum

✈️
Fly into Cancún (CUN) or Tulum (TQO)
Cancún is 2 hours north. Tulum's new international airport (TQO) is now open for select US flights.
🚌
ADO Bus from Cancún or Playa
Direct ADO buses connect all Riviera Maya destinations — under $15 USD.
🚗
Rental Car from Cancún
A rental car gives freedom to explore cenotes and Sian Ka'an. The 2-hour coastal drive is scenic and easy.

Insider Knowledge

Tips From Our Tulum Guides

🌅
Arrive at the ruins at sunrise
Ruins open at 8am. Arrive exactly at opening — by 9am tour buses arrive in force. First 45 minutes are magical.
🚴
Bike or tuk-tuk the cenote route
Gran Cenote, Dos Ojos, and others are within cycling distance of Tulum town.
🏛️
Hire a Mayan guide
Our local guides are descendants of Mayan communities and bring centuries of history to life.
🌿
Book Sian Ka'an early
Access to the biosphere reserve is regulated and strictly limited — book well in advance.
🏨
Stay in Tulum Pueblo
The town has great budget-friendly hotels and is only a 10-minute bike ride from everything.
🌙
No light pollution
Tulum's lack of heavy development means extraordinary stargazing — the Milky Way is visible on clear nights.
