About the Destination

The Pearl of the Pacific

Puerto Vallarta sits on the shores of Banderas Bay — Mexico's largest bay — cradled by the Sierra Madre mountains on one side and the warm Pacific on the other. The result is one of Mexico's most scenic and diverse adventure destinations.

From November through March, the bay becomes a nursery for humpback whales. Mothers and calves fill the waters just offshore, breaching and tail-slapping in full view.

Beyond the whales, the Sierra Madre jungle offers world-class hiking, zip-lining, river swimming, and waterfall expeditions.

Insider Knowledge

Tips From Our Puerto Vallarta Guides

🐋
Go small boat for whales
A small zodiac gets much closer to whales than large tour boats. Sol Seekers uses rigid inflatables with max 14 guests.
🌿
Book jungle tours in the morning
Morning temperatures are cooler, wildlife is more active, and the light for photos is spectacular.
🏖️
Playa las Ánimas by boat
Some of the most beautiful beaches south of Vallarta are only accessible by water taxi.
🌮
Eat on Calle Basilio Badillo
The 'Restaurant Row' of Zona Romántica — incredible variety from street tacos to upscale Mexican fusion.
🦟
Pack for the jungle
Long sleeves, bug spray, and closed-toe shoes are essential for jungle hikes.
🌊
Marietas permit required
The Marietas Islands require a permit — our tours handle this automatically.
