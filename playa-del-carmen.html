About the Destination

The Riviera Maya's Crown

Playa del Carmen is the beating heart of the Riviera Maya — a sophisticated beach town that perfectly balances adventure, culture, and natural wonder.

The Yucatán Peninsula beneath Playa is riddled with the world's largest network of underwater caves and cenotes — sacred sinkholes filled with crystal-clear freshwater fed by underground rivers.

Above ground, the vibrant Fifth Avenue pedestrian strip buzzes with world-class restaurants, boutiques, and live music. Everything from cave diving to yoga retreats is available within minutes of your hotel.

Getting There

How to Reach Playa del Carmen

✈️
Fly into Cancún (CUN)
Playa is 45 minutes south of Cancún airport by ADO bus or private transfer.
🚌
ADO Bus from Cancún
Direct ADO buses run every 20 minutes from Cancún airport — under $10 USD, fast and reliable.
⛴️
Ferry to Cozumel
The Cozumel ferry departs from Playa's pier every 1–2 hours. Easy to combine with a Playa stay.

Insider Knowledge

Tips From Our Playa del Carmen Guides

🌅
Cenotes are best at midday
Natural light shines directly into open-sky cenotes between 11am–2pm — creating magical rays of light through the water.
🚿
No sunscreen in cenotes
Cenotes require swimming without sunscreen to protect the delicate cave ecosystems. Shower before entering.
🌮
Best tacos are off 5th Ave
Walk one block west for half the price and double the authenticity.
💧
Stay hydrated in the jungle
The Yucatán humidity hits hard. Drink more water than you think you need on jungle tours.
🏖️
Playa Norte is quieter
The northern stretch of beach above 5th Avenue sees far fewer tourists — perfect for a calm morning swim.
🛵
Rent a scooter to explore
The coastal highway south toward Tulum is one of Mexico's most scenic rides.
